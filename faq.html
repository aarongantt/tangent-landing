The Basics

Tangent is a Chrome extension that brings AI to the page you're already reading. Highlight any text and a small Chip Bar appears — click a Chip to Summarize, Fact Check, Explain, Translate, or run 25+ other actions, and the answer opens in a floating chat anchored to the page. No tab-switching, no copy-paste, no losing your place.

Chatbots like ChatGPT and Claude live in their own tabs — you carry information to them. Sidebar extensions are mostly a chat window pinned to the edge of your browser, still waiting for you to paste something in. Tangent works the opposite way: it activates when you select something, so the AI already knows the context you care about. The chat opens next to the text, closes when you're done, and leaves nothing behind. You stay on the page.

How It Works

Tangent routes between four models based on your query's complexity. Fast Mode uses OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini and Anthropic's Claude Haiku — quick summaries, definitions, and everyday questions. Deep Mode uses OpenAI's GPT-4o and Anthropic's Claude Sonnet — complex analysis, long documents, and reasoning-heavy tasks. The router picks automatically; Deep Dive Chips always trigger Deep Mode. Deep queries cost more because the underlying models cost more.

When you highlight text and click a Chip, Tangent captures three things: the text you selected, a few surrounding paragraphs for context, and the page's title and URL. That's it — no full-page scraping, no reading other tabs by default, no persistent copy stored anywhere. The moment your answer returns, that context is gone. Multi-Tab Context is an opt-in setting for when you want Tangent to include content from other open tabs.

Prompt Chips are the one-click actions in the bar that appears when you highlight text — Summarize, Teach Me, Fact Check, Translate, Key Points, Pros/Cons, and around 25 others. Click one and the AI runs that specific instruction against your selection. You can create your own in Settings → Chips & Shortcuts: give it a name, a prompt template, and optionally a slash command like /rewrite, and it shows up alongside the built-in Chips.

Compatibility & Troubleshooting

Four common reasons:

  1. It's a restricted site — banking, healthcare, government, or legal pages auto-disable Tangent by design.
  2. The page uses a closed Shadow DOM (rare) — browser security prevents extensions from reaching the text.
  3. The extension hasn't been granted access to the page — check the extension icon for a permission prompt.
  4. The page's own JavaScript is intercepting the selection event — refresh the page and try again.

If none fit, click Send Feedback inside the extension with the URL and we'll investigate.

Chrome and Chromium-based browsers today — that includes Edge, Brave, Arc, Opera, and Vivaldi. Firefox and Safari are on the roadmap but aren't live yet.
